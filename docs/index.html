Intrapreneurship Features

Why become an intrapreneur?

Innovative

The intrapreneur looks for the opportunity in the company and capitalizes on it. He is the one who introduces new products and services in the market trying to fulfil customer and organization needs.

Productive

Curious and passionate intrapreneurs don’t want to waste time - so they find ways to manage their time effectively. Look for to-do lists, prioritisation of tasks, automation of mundane and repeatable tasks, the use of productivity apps, understanding when they’re most effective at certain tasks and executing accordingly and limiting their time spent in email.

Vision

Intrapreneurs see things most people don’t. This is due to the coalescence of a number of different traits - passion, broad interests, curiosity and associational thinking to name just a few.

Passionate

Innovation isn’t a part time or casual gig. Intrapreneurs must be passionate about their projects if they’re to carry them through all the various roadblocks, ask critical questions and apply critical thinking required to take ideas from zero to one.

Ethical

Ethics and integrity are the cornerstones of any successful business in the long term, therefore it is necessary that an intrapreneur upholds the letter of the law and the integrity of the business.

Well-Networked

Intrapreneur tend to have a network outside the organisation, which in turn helps them build broad experiences, demonstrates curiosity and also helps them accelerate their internal ventures through collaborations with the outside.

Paul Buchheit

One of the classic examples of an intrapreneur is Paul Buchheit, the man behind our mailbox – Google's Gmail, which in the early 2000s was in the news for its innovation in search and storage solutions. Buchheit is also known for his work on Google AdSense during his tenure at the internet company
