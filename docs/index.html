Intrapreneurship Features

Why become an intrapreneur?

Innovative

The intrapreneur looks for the opportunity in the company and capitalizes on it. He is the one who introduces new products and services in the market trying to fulfil customer and organization needs.

Productive

Curious and passionate intrapreneurs don’t want to waste time - so they find ways to manage their time effectively. Look for to-do lists, prioritisation of tasks, automation of mundane and repeatable tasks, the use of productivity apps, understanding when they’re most effective at certain tasks and executing accordingly and limiting their time spent in email.

Visionary

Intrapreneurs see things most people don’t. This is due to the coalescence of a number of different traits - passion, broad interests, curiosity and associational thinking to name just a few.

Passionate

Innovation isn’t a part time or casual gig. Intrapreneurs must be passionate about their projects if they’re to carry them through all the various roadblocks, ask critical questions and apply critical thinking required to take ideas from zero to one.

Ethical

Ethics and integrity are the cornerstones of any successful business in the long term, therefore it is necessary that an intrapreneur upholds the letter of the law and the integrity of the business.

Well-Networked

Intrapreneurs tend to have a network outside the organisation, which in turn helps them build broad experiences, demonstrates curiosity and also helps them accelerate their internal ventures through collaborations with the outside.

Paul Buchheit

One of the classic examples of an intrapreneur is Paul Buchheit, the man behind our mailbox – Google's Gmail, which in the early 2000s was in the news for its innovation in search and storage solutions. Buchheit is also known for his work on Google AdSense during his tenure at the internet company

Products

Gmail came about as part of Google's 80-20 time divide policy. In this work environment, employees are encouraged to spend 20% of their time in working on their own projects. Gmail came about through Paul's hard work and idea to have an email that could exceed the storage limit of 1GB.

Ideas to Encourage Intrapreneurship

Personal Growth Time

Increase Knowledge • 80-20 Time Divide

Companies can allot time during working hours for employees to work on personal projects or learn a new subject matter. Using innovative techniques similar to Google's 80-20 scheme, employees would be able to generate more ideas and thus, viable prodcuts, using an intrapreneurial approach to their work. Companies could also do well do allow their employess access to company-wide and external resources in order to achieve successful cases of intrapreneurship. Paul is proof that this method is beneficial.

Hackathons

Allow Innovation • Hacking Competitions

It has also been proven beneficial if the company's resources and products are permitted use to the employees during hackathons. An idea may be transformed into reality using a wide array of resources at an individual's disposal. Hackathons provide the time and space to create projects, either individually or with a group, that a company can then use internally or put out on the market depending on the type of product designed.

Freedom and Rewards

Reward Creativity • Ownership of Product

Allowing employees within an organization to have the freedom to try new things and empowering their ideas has proven to be successful. New ideas and technologies are constructed when employess are permitted the environment to follow their innovative sides rather than stick to the conventional guidelines of a company. Setting rewards for good projects also encourages workers to try and become innovative if they are not already. The best environment for a professional is an environment where they feel like they are able to grow and achieve new heights.
